Saultech Technology

Semiconductor Chip Cleaning Mechine

In this project, I designed the world's first semiconductor "chip" cleaning machine. I independently completed not only the mechanism but also process layout design and successfully verified the experiments for the related modules. Additionally, I applied for patents for the relevant inventions. Ultimately, I coordinated with other departments to finalize the development of the machine.

Bonding Inspection Machine

This is a hybrid bonding inspection machine. The principle involves using infrared light to penetrate silicon and assess the bonding results. The adjacent module is a tensile testing machine, which determines the force required to separate bonded chips or wafers. For this machine, I participated in the mechanical design, selection of optical components, assembly, and electrical distribution.

Particles Detecting Module

This is a module for detecting particles on wafers or chips, based on the principle of using a PMT to capture laser-scattered light reflected by particles. I was responsible for selecting the optical components, performing the mechanical design, and validating the module.

UV Debonding Machine

This is a simple UV debonding machine, for which I was responsible for both the design and assembly.

Bonding Machine


This is the hybrid bonder. At the time, and I was responsible for creating the demonstration animation for it.
